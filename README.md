# Consumer-Preference-Analysis-Local-Retailers-vs-Supershops-in-Bangladesh

Project Overview

This research project analyzes consumer behavior in Bangladesh’s evolving retail sector, focusing on why consumers prefer local retailers (Mudir Dokan & Kacha Bazar) over modern supershops.

The study applies the Theory of Planned Behavior (TPB) to identify the psychological and practical drivers influencing purchase decisions.

🎯 Objectives
Analyze consumer preference between local retailers and supershops
Apply TPB (Attitude, Subjective Norms, Perceived Behavioral Control)
Identify key factors influencing purchase intention
Provide data-driven business insights for retail strategy
🧠 Methodology
Research Type: Quantitative (Descriptive + Causal)
Sample Size: 350 respondents
Data Collection: Structured questionnaire (Google Forms)
Analysis Tools: Microsoft Excel, IBM SPSS (v27)
Techniques Used:
Descriptive Statistics
Reliability Analysis (Cronbach’s Alpha)
Multiple Regression Analysis
📈 Key Findings
The model explains 43.4% of purchase intention (R² = 0.434)
All TPB variables are statistically significant predictors
🔑 Key Drivers:
Attitude (β = 0.520) → Strongest factor
Freshness of products
Bargaining opportunities
Personal relationships
Perceived Behavioral Control (β = 0.261)
Proximity (walking distance)
Ease of transactions
Subjective Norms (β = 0.214)
Family & social influence
📉 Critical Insight

There is a noticeable gap between consumer intention and actual behavior, indicating the presence of hidden barriers in real-world shopping decisions.

💡 Business Implications
Local retailers maintain dominance due to trust, accessibility, and perceived freshness
Supershops need to improve:
Freshness perception
Pricing strategy
Accessibility in semi-urban areas
📊 Data Reliability
All constructs show strong reliability:
Cronbach’s Alpha > 0.83
